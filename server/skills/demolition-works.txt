Demolition Works Planning Skill

Use this guidance when the project scope is primarily demolition, strip-out, enabling works removal, or decommissioning.

Planning priorities:
- Identify demolition boundaries, retained structures, and protected assets.
- Sequence isolations, services disconnections, and make-safe activities before physical demolition.
- Include temporary works, access scaffolds, and exclusion zones where relevant.
- Separate hazardous material handling tasks (survey, removal, disposal, clearance) from general demolition.
- Include permits, regulatory notifications, and hold points for inspections.
- Structure work packages by area/zone, method, and disposal stream where possible.

Risk and controls emphasis:
- Public and workforce safety controls.
- Utility strike prevention and service verification.
- Dust, noise, vibration, and environmental management.
- Waste segregation, transport, and legal disposal records.

Output expectations:
- Use clear demolition-specific activity names.
- Keep dependencies explicit and realistic for staged deconstruction.
- Avoid introducing construction or installation scope unless explicitly requested.
