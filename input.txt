1. Project Definition 

1.1 Background 

[Company Name] operates a 20-mile drainage pipeline that connects the main plant facility to a downstream outflow point. The pipeline, originally installed [insert decade/year], has reached the end of its service life and is no longer required due to changes in plant operations and environmental regulations. 

The pipeline traverses both private and public land, including sections beneath public roads, rail lines, and utility corridors. Safe and compliant decommissioning of the asset is required to mitigate environmental risks, meet regulatory obligations, and release land for alternative use. 

1.2 Objectives 

Safely decommission and remove approximately 20 miles of redundant drainage pipeline. 

Comply with all relevant environmental, safety, and regulatory standards. 

Minimize disruption to public infrastructure and nearby communities. 

Restore affected areas to agreed conditions following removal. 

Provide project documentation, including as-built records and regulatory close-out reports. 

1.3 Deliverables 

Detailed decommissioning plan and regulatory approvals. 

Site mobilization and enabling works. 

Physical removal of pipeline, supporting structures, and associated fittings. 

Environmental management (waste disposal, contamination testing, ground remediation where required). 

Restoration of impacted land, roads, and infrastructure. 

Final project report and regulatory close-out certification. 

Shape 

2. Project Assumptions 

Necessary permits and approvals (environmental, planning, road opening, utility clearances) can be obtained within standard lead times. 

Public authorities (local councils, highways, utilities) will provide required access and coordination. 

Pipeline has been drained, cleaned, and isolated from plant operations prior to removal. 

No hazardous or unknown materials remain in the pipeline (only minor residual waste requiring safe disposal). 

Land access agreements with private landowners will be arranged before physical works commence. 

Disposal facilities for pipeline materials (scrap metal, soil, contaminated waste if present) are available within regional distance. 

Shape 

3. Known Risks 

Environmental Risks: Potential soil or groundwater contamination discovered during excavation. 

Public Infrastructure Risks: Damage to roads, utilities, or transport infrastructure during removal. 

Health & Safety Risks: Risks to workers from confined spaces, heavy lifting, or contaminated materials. 

Regulatory Risks: Delays in obtaining required permits or non-compliance with environmental standards. 

Community Impact Risks: Noise, dust, traffic disruption, or public complaints during works. 

Cost/Schedule Risks: Discovery of unforeseen conditions (e.g., undocumented crossings, poor soil stability). 

Access Risks: Delays in securing landowner agreements or restricted access to sensitive areas.
